Our Story

Building a Greener Philippines

We believe every Filipino family deserves access to clean, reliable, and affordable energy.

Our Mission

Solar House was founded in 2018 with a clear mission: to make solar energy accessible to every Filipino household. We saw how rising electricity costs burdened families and knew that the Philippines — blessed with over 300 sunny days a year — had untapped potential for solar power.

Today, we serve over 5,000 homes across Luzon, Visayas, and Mindanao, from studio condos in BGC to family homes in Davao.

Community-Centered Approach

We don't just sell solar panels — we partner with barangays, cooperatives, and local governments to educate communities about renewable energy. Our free Solar Literacy workshops have reached over 20,000 Filipinos in 150+ communities.

Every installation is handled by our certified Filipino technicians who understand local building codes, typhoon-proofing requirements, and the unique needs of tropical architecture.

Sustainability Impact

Together with our customers, we've offset over 15,000 tons of carbon emissions — the equivalent of planting 750,000 trees. Our goal is to help the Philippines reach 35% renewable energy by 2030.

We also run a panel recycling program ensuring end-of-life panels are disposed of responsibly, keeping toxic materials out of Philippine landfills.

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about going solar in the Philippines.

On average, Solar House customers reduce their monthly electricity bills by 30-50%. A typical 3kW system for a household spending ₱8,000-₱12,000/month on electricity can save ₱3,000-₱5,000 monthly. With net metering, excess energy you produce gets credited to your next bill.
Yes! Solar panels still generate electricity on cloudy and rainy days, though at reduced capacity (typically 25-50% of peak output). The Philippines receives enough annual sunlight that seasonal variations average out. Our systems are designed with this in mind, and battery storage ensures you have power even during extended overcast periods.
Most Philippine roofs — whether concrete, galvanized iron (yero), or tile — can support solar panels. Each panel weighs approximately 20kg, and our mounting systems distribute weight evenly. During our free site assessment, our engineers evaluate your roof's structural integrity, angle, and orientation to ensure safe and optimal installation.
Absolutely. Our panels are IEC 61215 certified and tested to withstand wind speeds up to 200 km/h. We use reinforced mounting brackets specifically designed for Philippine typhoon conditions. In our 6 years of operation, zero panels have been damaged by typhoons across all 5,000+ installations.
Net metering is a program by Philippine distribution utilities (like Meralco) that allows you to sell excess solar energy back to the grid. When your panels produce more electricity than you use, the surplus goes to the grid and you receive credits on your bill. We handle the entire net metering application process for you at no extra charge.
Physical installation takes 1-3 days depending on system size. The complete process from site assessment to activation takes about 2-4 weeks, which includes engineering design, permit processing, installation, inspection, and utility interconnection. We handle all paperwork and coordination with your local utility.
